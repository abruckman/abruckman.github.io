The Life of a Fisheries Observer

What is a Fisheries Observer?

A fisheries observer is a scientist or surveyor contracted by a fisheries regulator to monitor the activity of a fishing vessel. An observer's work varies from taking catch data, to recording mammal sitings and much more.

As an observer, you are both the scientific community and the federal government's man on the ground for better or worse.

Where did you work?

I worked on boats out of Dutch Harbor, Alaska. If you look at the aleutian Island chain, Dutch Harbor is on Unalaska, the 4th or so big island out.

I worked on all sorts of boats,catching all kinds of fish, with crews ranging from 4 to more than one hundred. The Defender (above), had a crew of 32.
